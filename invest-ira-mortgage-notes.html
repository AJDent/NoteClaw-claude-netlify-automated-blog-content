How to Invest My Retirement Account in Real Estate

Can You Really Invest Your IRA in Mortgage Notes?

Yes. And people are doing it right now — earning 12–30%+ annual returns on mortgage notes, completely tax-deferred or tax-free, inside their retirement accounts.

If you've got an IRA or old 401(k) sitting in mutual funds earning 7% in a good year, this might be the most important thing you read this month.

The Problem With Traditional Retirement Accounts

Your 401(k) or IRA at Fidelity, Schwab, or Vanguard has a dirty little secret: they only let you invest in what they sell.

What Is a Self-Directed IRA (SDIRA)?

A Self-Directed IRA is an IRA held at a custodian that allows you to invest in alternative assets — including mortgage notes.

How to Set Up an SDIRA for Note Investing

1. Choose a Custodian. 2. Fund the Account (Rollover). 3. Buy Notes.

The Rules

No self-dealing, no disqualified persons, no personal use.
